Case Study: Phishing Attack

Phishing is a type of cybercrime in which attackers trick individuals into revealing sensitive information such as passwords, bank details, or OTPs. It usually happens through fake emails, messages, or websites that appear to be from trusted organizations like banks or companies.

A typical phishing attack begins when the victim receives a message or email that looks legitimate. It may ask the user to click on a link to verify their account or claim a reward. Once the user clicks the link, they are redirected to a fake website that looks similar to the original one. The user is then asked to enter personal information such as login credentials or financial details. This information is directly captured by the attacker.

Students, elderly people, and individuals who are not aware of such scams are usually targeted. The consequences can be serious, including financial loss, identity theft, and unauthorized access to personal accounts.

To prevent phishing attacks, users should avoid clicking on suspicious links, verify the sender’s authenticity, and never share sensitive information online. Awareness and careful behavior are essential to stay safe from such cyber threats.
