Digital Communication Report

Poor digital communication can sometimes lead to serious problems. For example, a student might send a message to a professor using informal language or unclear wording, which could be misunderstood as disrespectful. This can negatively affect the student’s impression and communication with the professor. In another situation, sharing incorrect information on social media without verifying it can spread confusion among people.

Such problems can be avoided by maintaining professionalism and clarity while communicating online. It is important to use polite language, proper grammar, and clear sentences, especially in academic or professional communication. Additionally, verifying information before sharing and being mindful of tone can prevent misunderstandings. Responsible digital communication helps in building a positive image and avoids unnecessary conflicts.
